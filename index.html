EGAN: A Lightweight Vocoder with a Multi-Channeled Excitation Generator and a Linear Time-Varying Filter

Abstract:

In this paper, we revisited and improved our previous proposed model EGAN, a pitch controllable vocoder based on the source-filter model. The source model is a trainable channeled neural excitation generator guided by harmonic and noise inputs: F0-aligned sine-cosine harmonics plus modified velvet noise jointly modeling periodic and aperiodic components while preserving pitch control. The filter part is simply a linear time-varying filter based on STFT and windowed overlap-add (WOLA). We analyze aliasing and distortion across window types and lengths, derive practical non-aliasing criteria and window-length guidelines, and use them to set filtering hyper-parameters. The training adopts a generative adversarial network (GAN) objective based on the least-squares slicing adversarial network (LS-SAN) formulation, rather than the least-squares GAN (LS-GAN), improving convergence stability and fidelity. In a multi-speaker copy synthesis task on the CMU ARCTIC speech corpus, EGAN attains higher listener preference than our prior prototype. In a speaker-independent task on the LibriTTS-R speech corpus, it achieves a performance competitive with SiFiGAN while remaining compact (1.9M parameters) and capable of real-time inference on a single GPU. EGAN thus offers an interpretable, pitch-controllable alternative to purely data-driven waveform synthesizers, pairing a simple deterministic filter with a purposefully adversarially trained excitation branch.
